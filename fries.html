French Fries

Ingredients:

  1. Potatoes (russet or Idaho are good choices)
  2. Vegetable oil for frying
  3. Salt to taste

Instructions:

Peel the potatoes and cut them into evenly sized sticks, about 1/4 to 1/2 inch thick.
Soak the cut potatoes in cold water for about 30 minutes to remove excess starch. This helps in achieving a crispier fry.
Cook the toor dal in a pressure cooker with enough water until soft and mushy.
In a separate pot, heat oil and add mustard seeds. Let them splutter.
Add cumin seeds, asafoetida, and curry leaves. Saute for a few seconds.
Drain the potatoes and pat them dry with paper towels.
Heat the vegetable oil in a deep fryer or a large pot to around 350°F (175°C).
Carefully add the potatoes to the hot oil in batches, ensuring not to overcrowd the fryer or pot. Fry for about 5-7 minutes or until they are golden brown and crispy.
Remove the fries from the oil using a slotted spoon or tongs and place them on a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil. Sprinkle the fries with salt while they are still hot.
Serve immediately and enjoy your homemade French fries!
Note: Be cautious when working with hot oil and never leave it unattended
