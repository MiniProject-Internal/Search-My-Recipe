Idli Sambhar

Ingredients For Idli:

  1. 2 cups idli rice
  2. 1 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  3. Salt to taste
  4. Water as needed

For Sambhar:

  1. 1 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas)
  2. 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, beans, potatoes, etc.), chopped
  3. 1 onion, chopped
  4. 2 tomatoes, chopped
  5. 2-3 green chilies, slit
  6. 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  7. 1 tablespoon sambhar powder
  8. 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  9. 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  10. A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  11. Few curry leaves
  12. 2 tablespoons oil
  13. Salt to taste
  14. Water as needed
  15. Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions For Idli:

Wash and soak the idli rice and urad dal separately for 4-6 hours.
Grind the soaked urad dal to a smooth batter consistency using some water. Transfer it to a large bowl.
Grind the soaked rice to a slightly coarse texture. Add this to the bowl with the urad dal batter.
Add salt to the batter and mix well. Allow it to ferment overnight or for 8-10 hours.
Grease the idli molds with oil and pour the batter into them. Steam in an idli steamer for about 10-12 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the idlis comes out clean. Remove from heat and set aside.

For Sambhar:

Cook the toor dal in a pressure cooker with enough water until soft and mushy.
In a separate pot, heat oil and add mustard seeds. Let them splutter.
Add cumin seeds, asafoetida, and curry leaves. Saute for a few seconds.
Add chopped onions and saut√© until translucent.
Add chopped tomatoes and green chilies. Cook until tomatoes are soft.
Add chopped vegetables, tamarind paste, sambhar powder, and salt. Mix well.
Add water as needed to cover the vegetables and let it come to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer until vegetables are cooked through.
Once the vegetables are cooked, add the cooked toor dal and mix well. Adjust the consistency by adding more water if necessary.
Let the sambhar simmer for a few more minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
Garnish with fresh coriander leaves before serving.
Serve hot idlis with piping hot sambhar for a delicious and comforting meal. You can also serve with coconut chutney for extra flavor. Enjoy your homemade Idli Sambhar!
